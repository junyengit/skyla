Feature 01

360° Views of
Los Angeles

Look north and you'll find the iconic Hollywood Sign nestled in the Santa Monica Mountains. Shift east and the Downtown LA skyline rises above the basin in glass and steel. Look south across the endless city grid. Spin west to catch Century City glittering on the horizon.

On clear days, visibility stretches for miles — from the San Gabriel Mountains across the basin to the Westside. Every compass point offers a postcard.

Feature 02

20-Foot
Soaring Ceilings

Step off the elevator and feel the space open up. Skyla's penthouse level features dramatic 20-foot ceilings that transform the entire floor into something closer to an art installation than a room.

The sheer volume of the space creates an atmosphere unlike any other rooftop experience in the city — airy, light-filled, and utterly unlike the cramped venues you've come to expect.

Feature 03

Floor-to-Ceiling
Windows

Every wall is a window. Skyla's curtain-glass facade wraps the entire penthouse floor in uninterrupted views — no columns, no obstructions, no compromises. The city is always visible, from every seat.

Whether you're settled into the curved lounge seating or standing at the glass, the boundary between inside and the LA skyline all but disappears. Sunrise or sunset, day or night — it's always a spectacle.

Feature 04

Two Large
Outdoor Patios

Step through the glass and onto one of Skyla's two expansive outdoor terraces. Each patio is oriented to give you the most dramatic possible angle on Los Angeles — completely open-air, completely unobstructed.

The East Patio faces the Downtown skyline, perfect for morning visits and blue-sky shots of the city's towers. The West Patio catches the sunset as it drops behind Century City and spills gold across the western sky — the most photographed view in the building.
